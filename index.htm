INTRODUCTION

Viscosity is the property of a moving fluid which shows the tendency of the fluid to resist flow. Newton formulated a law called NEWTON'S LAW OF VISCOSITY which mathematically states that :-
τ = μ ∂u/∂y , where 'τ' is shear stress, 'μ' is coefficient of viscosity and u is velocity.
The dimensional formula of coefficient of viscosity is ML-1T-1.



MOMENTUM DIFFUSIVITY

The term 'μ' is dynamic viscosity(also known as absolute viscosity). But the term KINEMATIC VISCOSITY is used more often. It is also called momentum diffusivity. It is mathematically defined as ratio of dynamic viscosity and density.
Momentum diffusivity(ν) = μ/ρ , where ρ is the density.
It is defined as the ability of the fluid to transport momentum.

TYPES OF FLUID

(i)Newtonian Fluid- The fluids whose coefficient of viscosity(μ) remains constant at a particular temperature are called Newtonian fluids. When a graph between shear stress(τ) and rate of deformation for such a fluid is drawn, it comes to be a straight line with μ as its slope. Usually simple olecules form Newtonian fluids because they can move easily on applying small amount of stress.
(ii)Non-Newtonian Fluid- The fluids which do not obey Newton's law of viscosity are called Non-Newtonian fluids. Viscosity of such fluids is dependent on shear rate.

EFFECTS ON VISCOSITY

For liquids, as the temperature increases, viscosity decreases. For gases, as temperature increases, viscosity also increases.
Usually, viscosity of both liquids and gases is independent of pressure.

APPLICATIONS

Knowing the viscosity helps engineers know how a fluid will behave under different circumstances. Engineers also use this equation when designing devices. By using a fluid with a known viscosity and applying a force to it, engineers can calculate how fast the fluid will move. Some of the important uses of viscosity in various fields are as follows:-

  • Blood circulation through arteries and veins depends upon the viscosity of fluids.
  • The oil drop experiment done by Milikan was possible due to the known value of viscosity of oil.
  • Different fluids are used as lubricant in machines depending upon their viscosity at different temperatures.
  • The viscosity of paints, varnishes, and comparable household products is closely regulated so that the paints and varnishes can be applied smoothly and evenly with a brush roller.
  • Drug companies manufacture medicines, such as cough syrup, that have a high viscosity yet are still drinkable, in order to coat and soothe the throat.

KEY FACTS ABOUT VISCOSITY

  • Viscosity is used as a way to predict when volcanoes erupt. When the lava comes out very thickly (viscous), there is more chance that it will erupt violently.
  • The word viscous comes from the Latin root viscum, meaning sticky.
  • Viscosity of an ideal gas is 0.
  • Viscosity is measured with an instrument called Viscometer
  • Viscosity is undefined for a static fluid.
  • High molecular weight polymers can have viscosity values in the millions of centipoises range.
  • Viscosity of gases is of order 10-3.
